# First IC :)

_Homemade computer chips / integrated circuits_

### Preface

I am very excited to announce details of the first homemade integrated circuit and share the journey that this project has taken me on over the past year. I hope that my success will inspire others and help start a revolution in home chip fabrication. When I set out on this project I had no idea of what I had gotten myself into, but in the end I learned more than I ever thought I would about physics, chemistry, optics, electronics, and so many other fields. Furthermore, my efforts have only been matched with the most positive feedback and support from the world; I owe a sincere thanks to everyone who has helped me, given me advice, and inspired me on this project. Especially my amazing parents, who not only support and encourage me in any way they can but also give me a space to work in and put up with the electricity costs… Thank you!

Let’s democratize the tools of innovation.

[![DSC_8683](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/DSC_8683-1024x678.jpg)](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/DSC_8683.jpg) I present the first homemade (lithographically-fabricated) integrated circuit – the “Z1” PMOS dual differential amplifier chip. I say “lithographically-fabricated” because Jeri Ellsworth made the first transistors and logic gates (meticulously hand wired with conductive epoxy) and showed the world that this is possible. Inspired by her work, I have demonstrated ICs made by a scalable, industry-standard, photolithographic process. Needless to say, this is the logical step-up from my [previous replication of Jeri’s FET fabrication work.](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/fet/)

### Design

[I designed the Z1 amplifier](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/ic-design-tools-from-verilog-to-mask/) looking for a simple chip to test and tweak my process. Layout was done in [Magic VLSI](http://opencircuitdesign.com/magic/) for a 4 mask PMOS process (active/doped area, gate oxide, contact window, and top metal.) PMOS has advantages over NMOS as far as mobile ionic contamination that lends it to being fabricated in a garage. The masks are designed in 16:9 aspect ratio for easy projection.

 [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/Screen-Shot-2018-03-02-at-9.58.40-PM-300x188.png)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/Screen-Shot-2018-03-02-at-9.58.40-PM.png)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/Screen-Shot-2018-03-02-at-9.56.27-PM-300x188.png)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/Screen-Shot-2018-03-02-at-9.56.27-PM.png)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/1active-300x169.png)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/1active.png)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/2gate-300x169.png)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/2gate.png)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/3contact-300x169.png)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/3contact.png)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/4metal-300x169.png)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/4metal.png)

The feature (gate) size is approximately 175μm although there are test features as small as 2μm on the chip. Each amplifier section (center and right) contain 3 transistors (2 for [long-tailed differential pair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Differential_amplifier) and one as current source/load resistor) which means a total of 6 FETs on the IC. The left portion of the IC contains resistors, capacitors, diodes, and other test features used to characterize the fabrication process. Each node of the differential pairs is broken out to a separate pin on the lead frame so it can be analyzed and external biasing can be added as necessary.

[![Functional diagram](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Screen-Shot-2019-07-11-at-10.36.44-PM-e1563298696731-222x300.png)](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Screen-Shot-2019-07-11-at-10.36.44-PM-e1563298696731.png) Functional diagram

EDIT: see update at the bottom, the transistor gate length has been reduced to <5µm (1975 tech. level) which brings an increase in device performance.

### Fabrication

There are 66 individual fabrication steps to make this chip and it takes approximately 12 hours for a full run. The process yield can be as high as 80% for these large features, but is largely dependent on my coffee intake that day. I have also made Youtube videos covering [semiconductor fabrication theory](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qCSIGejNT4M&t=1808s) and [discrete MOSFET fabrication](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s1MCi7FliVY).

The home chip fab chemistry bench is pictured below and basically includes everything needed to manufacture ICs except a vacuum chamber and a lithography setup. More information about equipment and chemicals is in my [Supercon talk from 2018.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=23fTB3hG5cA)

 [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/59321358854__620F190B-636B-4833-886D-81F2F512E735-e1571534174252-1024x819.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/59321358854__620F190B-636B-4833-886D-81F2F512E735-e1571534174252.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/IMG_7975-e1571534091789-1024x819.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/IMG_7975-e1571534091789.jpg)

50mm <100> orientation Silicon wafers with bulk resistivity 1 to 10 Ω-cm (30.8 to 308 Ω/sq for thickness of 325µm) are scored into 5.08 x 3.175mm dies (~16mm^2 area) with an [Epilog fiber laser.](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/laser-silicon-wafer-scribing-and-cutting/) Polyvinyl Alcohol in water or photoresist can be spun on the wafer prior to laser scribing to “catch” laser ablation debris and the film is later removed in solvent before processing. This die size is chosen to fit into a Kyocera 24pin DIP carrier.

 [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Da8vGT4UQAA-TPo-300x197.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/Da8vGT4UQAA-TPo.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8257-300x199.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8257.jpg)

Native oxide is stripped off the wafer with a quick dilute HF dip and then they are extensively cleaned in Piranha solution (H2SO4:H2O2), RCA 1 (H2O:NH3:H2O2), RCA 2 (H2O:HCL:H2O2), followed by another dilute HF dip. Most of these cleaning dips are for 10 minutes and can be facilitated by raising to ~40ºC.

The field oxide is [thermally grown](http://cleanroom.byu.edu/OxideTimeCalc) in a water vapor ambient (wet oxidation) to a thickness of 5000-8000Å. One may consider mixing the DI water for this step with a few percent HCl. The Chrloine atoms help getter and immobilize ionic contaminants and are also said to increase the growth rate by 5-7%. Together with the fact I am making PMOS devices rather than NMOS, these give a huge edge over contamination control and allow decently preforming devices to be fabricated in a garage.

 [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V7881-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V7881.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V7882-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V7882.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V7877-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V7877.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8176-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8176.jpg)

The oxidized wafer is ready for patterning of the active/doped (P-type) area. Positive photoresist ( [AZ MiR 701](https://www.microchemicals.com/micro/mir7011.pdf) for SiO2 patterning and [AZ 4210](https://www.microchemicals.com/micro/tds_az_p4000_series.pdf) for Al layer) is spun on at around 3000rpm yielding a film of about 1.5μm for the AZ MiR 701 or 3.5μm for the AZ 4210 which is soft baked at 90C on a hotplate.

[Lithography process details](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/developing-patterning-process-for-homemade-microelectronics/)

The active area mask is exposed with my [Mark IV maskless photolithography stepper](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/mark-iv-maskless-photolithography-stepper/) at 365nm UV and the pattern is developed in TMAH or KOH solution depending on the resist.

[![Resist pattern](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8279-1024x678.jpg)](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8279.jpg) Resist pattern [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8206-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8206.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8203-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8203.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8214-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8214.jpg)

The resist pattern is then hard baked and a [number of other tricks](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/sio2-patterning/) are used to ensure good resist adhesion and chemical stability during the following HF etch step which transfers that pattern to the oxide layer and opens windows to the bare silicon surface for doping. These regions later become the source/drain of the FETs.

 [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/D7V7331-e1524952531619-1024x766.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/D7V7331-e1524952531619.jpg)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8361-e1524952474139-1024x765.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8361-e1524952474139.jpg)

Doping is then carried out by either solid or liquid source. The solid source is a Boron Nitride disk that is placed in proximity (<2mm) from the wafer in the tube furnace. Alternatively, spin-on liquid sources can be prepared from Phosphoric or Boric acid in water or solvents and doping is carried out in a standard [pre-deposition/HF dip/drive-in/deglaze process](http://desertsilicon.com/spin-on-glass-spin-on-dopants-application-procedure/). I obtained Phosphoric acid in pure form on Amazon and Boric acid from Roach & Ant killer. Since the starting wafer for PMOS here is N-type, I am doing P diffusions of Boron for the source/drain regions and am targeting a sheet resistance in diffused regions of 100 to 250 Ω/sq.

The above mentioned patterning steps are then repeated twice for the gate oxide layer and then the contact layer. The gate oxide must be much thinner (<~750Å) than the field oxide, so the regions between the source/drain are etched away and a thinner oxide is grown there. Then, since the whole wafer has been oxidized during the doping step, contact windows must be etched for the metal layer to make connection with source/drain doped regions.

[![na_1](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/na_1.png)](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/na_1.png)

 [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4752-1024x674.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4752.jpg) [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4754-1024x672.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4754.jpg) [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4755-1024x663.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4755.jpg) [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4756-1024x652.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4756.jpg) [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4760-1024x656.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_4760.jpg)

Now, all the transistors are formed and are ready to be interconnected and broken out to the lead frame. A blanket layer of Aluminum (400-500nm) is [sputtered](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/sem-sample-preparation-dc-sputtering/) or [thermally evaporated](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/thermal-evaporation-physical-vapor-deposition/) onto the wafer. An alternative would be to use the [lift-off process](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/lift-off-metal-patterning/) in which the photoresist is patterned first and then metal is deposited. To support wire bonding, this metal layer is made thicker (around 2.5µm for Au wire wedge bonding.) These films have a measured bulk resistivity around 5.4e-6 Ω-cm for thermally evaporated films, double the ideal value of 2.7e-6 Ω-cm for Al at 20ºC. The incorporation of Oxygen and other gasses into the Al film during vacuum deposition likely accounts for this difference.

 [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8368-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/_d7v8368/)  [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_3343-e1524919489448-1024x678.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/img_3343/)

The metal layer is then patterned with photolithography and etched in hot Phosphoric acid (50ºC) to yield the completed IC. The final steps before testing are visual inspection and high temperature annealing of the Aluminum to create ohmic connections.

[![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8327-300x199.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8327.jpg) [![](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8372-300x199.jpg)](https://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/D7V8372.jpg)

[![IMG_3212](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_3212-1024x768.jpg)](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_3212.jpg)

The finished chip is now ready for packaging and testing.

### Testing

I don’t have a wire bonder (accepting donations!) so my testing right now is limited to manually probing the wafer with sharp tweezers or using a flip-chip board (difficult to align) to connect it to a curve tracer. The differential amplifier is also tested empirically in-circuit to verify operation.

As you can see above in the PMOS FET Id vs. Vds curves, there is lots of die to die variation and devices made on the same day can have widely different characteristics. Taking 5 traces with -1V Vgs increment requires about a -8V body/substrate bias to overcome fixed charges (positive impurity ions trapped under gate) and lattice defects in the gate region and yield the expected graph.

Electrical characteristics of Al/Si junctions are characterized as well and show the expected results. We can create three such basic contacts between Aluminum and Silicon. Aluminum is P-type with respect to Silicon so Schottky diodes are formed whenever Aluminum comes into contact with lightly doped N Silicon. Sometimes my devices showed a tunneling characteristic rather than the expected diode, so I theorize that if the same device is processed for a longer time under high temperatures (>1000ºC), increased oxidation at the Si surface causes the Phosphorous at the surface of the wafer to “pile-up” because of the increased solubility of N-type dopant in SiO2. This creates an “N+” region at the surface and the higher dopant concentration creates a diminishing depletion layer which relates to a small potential energy barrier (the electrons can easily tunnel across it), explaining the symmetrical IV curve.

Additionally, the gate oxide dielectric breakdown voltage can be destructively tested. For high quality SiO2, this should be a little over 1V/nm and is easily tested by sweeping Vgs up from 0V and noticing when a large current flows (in normal operation the gate is insulating and no current should be able to flow).

[![IMG_7198](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_7198-1024x744.jpg)](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_7198.jpg)

This plot shows gate dielectric breakdown occurring at 21.7V for a 25nm thick gate device, indicating a decent thermally grown oxide quality which could be improved by being grown in an atmosphere with higher Nitrogen content.

The switching and differential amplifier characteristics can also be demonstrated. The trace on the right shows the output of the chip configured as a fully differential amplifier, mixing (adding/subtracting) a 1kHz and 50kHz sine waves together.

The final characteristic to test for is a low-leakage, fully insulating gate as one of the main requirements of true MOSFET operation. As you can see, I am able to charge up the gate of the device and turn it on through a high impedance connection through my fingertip, and the 1, 0 states of the FET are “latching” due to charge staying on the gate of the FETs and having no pathway to dissipate.

[http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG\_7253.m4v](http://sam.zeloof.xyz/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/IMG_7253.m4v)

Long ago, some amplifiers were plagued with “popcorn” or burst noise, thought to be caused from random events in defects within the semiconductor. This manifests as large step-impulse changes in the output and has been virtually eliminated in modern ICs due to improved material purity and processing cleanliness. However, some of the devices I made exhibit tons of popcorn noise, shown in the video below (noise in differential pair is amplified on scope, zero input creates hundreds of millivolt output). One of my favorite quotes on this type of noise was said by an engineer in reference to the MAX9776, “You could measure it with a frog’s leg and a stopwatch.” Mine clearly falls in this category…
