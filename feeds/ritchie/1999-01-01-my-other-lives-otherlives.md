# My Other Lives

My other lives

Outside of my main professional career, I have accumulated other
WWW-recorded accomplishments and have other interests. Generally I
pursue these interests using separate mail addresses, SS#, and
DNA.

- Although my professional truck-driving career was brief, I did
  learn enough to do a nearly perfect double-clutch with a
  non-synchromesh transmission, while driving up a hill on a
  forest track, carrying a bunch of kids in the back of a
  dumptruck--the tippable, flat-bed kind, not one designed to
  squash garbage.

This talent (and general cautiousness) seemed sufficient to garner
a nominal association with the

Swift Transportation company as safety director.

- Perhaps this position was gained by

being a big-rig transporter driver of NASCAR autos to and from
races for the Roush Racing/Valvoline team.

- I parlayed a stint on the High School AV squad into a side

career as recording engineer for rising C&W singers in Nashville,
for example Jill Anne Curington.

- Similar C&W engineering and artistic talent was expressed in
  Brazil as well: I wish you could still see

www.par-ou-impar.com.br/dennis.htm, which captured a career as
"Diretor/produtor e engenheiro musical, além de cantor,
arranjador, músico, pintor, cozinheiro, motorista, mágico,
humorista.... Trabalha com os mais importantes artístas da música
POP e Country, seu talento sem dávida trouxe a este projeto a mais
alta qualidade." All too brief a career, I'm afraid, since the
link is now dead.

- Although my connection with local politics, particularly those
  of Republicans in Carlstadt, NJ, has traditionally been tenuous,
  and I'm afraid that my own attendance at town council meetings
  is worse than desultory, nevertheless there was a successful
  reelection in 1999. I can assure you that Dennis Ritchie in the
  role of Recreation Director remains utterly free of any taint of
  nepotism in connection with Paul Ritchie's appointment as
  Recycling Director.

- I lost an election a while ago in a Kentucky Equine Law group,
  but seem to maintain some agricultural roots as Poultry Division
  chairman of the

Arkansas Farm Bureau

- It really is a good idea to

carry medical insurance ID and be prepared to describe any special
conditions you have, and I guess this is true in northern Nevada
as much as everywhere.
