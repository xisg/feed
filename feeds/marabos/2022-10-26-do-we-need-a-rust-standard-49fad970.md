# Do we need a "Rust Standard"?

Languages like C and C++ are _standardized_.
They are fully specified in an internationally recognized standards document.
Languages like Python, Swift and Rust do not have such a standards document.

Should Rust be standardized? Why, or why not?
In this blog post, I try to explain why I do think we need an accurate _specification_,
why I do _not_ think we need “standardization” (depending on your definition),
and give an overview of the current state of Rust’s stability and specification efforts.
