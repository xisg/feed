# About danluu.com

### About The Blog

This started out as a way to jot down thoughts on areas that seem interesting but underappreciated. Since then, this site has grown to the point where it gets [millions of hits a month](https://danluu.com/blog-ads/#update) and I see that it's commonly cited by professors in their courses and on stackoverflow.

That's flattering, but more than anything else, I view that as a sign there's a desperate shortage of [understandable explanation of technical topics](//danluu.com/teach-debugging/). There's nothing here that most of my co-workers don't know (with the exception of maybe three or four posts where I propose novel ideas). It's just that they don't blog and I do. I'm not going to try to [convince you to start writing a blog](https://sites.google.com/site/steveyegge2/you-should-write-blogs), since that has to be something you want to do, but I will point out that there's a large gap that's waiting to be filled by your knowledge. When I started writing this blog, I figured almost no one would ever read it; sure Joel Spolsky and Steve Yegge created widely read blogs, but that was back when almost no one was blogging. Now that there are millions of blogs, there's just no way to start a new blog and get noticed. Turns out that's not true.

This site also archives a few things that have fallen off the internet, like [this history of subspace, the 90s video game](//danluu.com/subspace-history/), the [su3su2u1 introduction to physics](//danluu.com/su3su2u1/physics/), [the su3su2u1 review of hpmor](//danluu.com/su3su2u1/hpmor/), [Dan Weinreb's history of Symbolics and Lisp machines](//danluu.com/symbolics-lisp-machines/), [this discussion of open vs. closed social networks](//danluu.com/open-social-networks/), [this discussion about the differences between SV and Boston, and Stanford and MIT](//danluu.com/mit-stanford/), [the comp.programming.threads FAQ](//danluu.com/threads-faq/), and [this presentation about Microsoft culture from 2000](//danluu.com/microsoft-culture/) [.](//danluu.com/mcilroy-unix)

P.S. If you enjoy this blog, you'd probably enjoy [RC](https://www.recurse.com/scout/click?t=b504af89e87b77920c9b60b2a1f6d5e8), which I've heard called "nerd camp for programmers".
